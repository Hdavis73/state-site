Fredericksburg, Texas

This little suburban city is located in central Texas, roughly an hour and a half outside of the state capital. Fredericksburg Texas is small and mighty, with a population of 11,257 and an immense amount of interesting history behind it. Fredericksburg was founded by German immigrant Baron Otfried Hans von Meusebach (History Fredericksburg, TX), and holds pride in its German roots, housing all of its history and teaching about German heritage in the famous Pioneer Meuseum (Pioneer Home Page). If you're looking for a smaller town to travel to with lots of rich history to learn about, put Fredericksburg at the top of your list.

Did You Know?

Fredericksburg hosted the largest group of soldiers in the civil war (10 facts: Fredericksburg, 2021).

Texas is generally pretty flat, but Fredericksburg is located in what's considered the Hill Country, a part of the country with gorgeous hills and cliffs.

Fredericksburg is a sister city to Montabaur, Germany (Sister City, Fredericksburg).

Fredericksburg has made many "top" lists, including "Best Small Town" (5 fast facts about Fredericksburg, 2022).

Fredericksburg was incorporated in 1846 (History, Fredericksburg).

Interesting History
City Name

Fredericksburg was originally named Friedrichsburg but was changed to the modern spelling in honor of Prince Frederick of Prussia (History, Fredericksburg).

City Founders

Fredericksburg was founded by Baron Otfried Hans von Meusebach, who worked in administrative positions in a multitude of countries before the Society for the Protection of German Immigrants in Texas appointed him commissioner general, giving him the opportunity to travel on horseback from Galveston Texas to New Braunfels with his small entourage (Texas State Historical Association, 2020).

Walk Through Tour

If you're interested in visiting Fredericksburg and learning more about the town's rich history, the town offers a Walk Through Tour for visitors. On this tour, you'll get to see the town's museums, historical restaurants, churches, and more as your guide explains all about the city's culture and roots in-depth (Fredericksburg Convention & Visitor Bureau, 2019).
