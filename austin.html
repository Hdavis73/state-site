Austin, Texas

Located in central Texas, the city of Austin may be the capital of Texas, but you'll find much more than just country hoedowns in this city. Austin is well known for being the "Live Music Capital of the World", with plenty of popular live music venues to choose from. During the day, Austin is all about hustle and bustle, with people from all walks of life traveling to and fro to work or shop, but at night, especially on Fridays and Saturdays, 6th Street is where it's at. A walk down 6th street on a weekend night will present you with guitarists up and down the way, food vendors, food trucks, and the roar of live music in all the many popular nightclubs. This kind of nightlife is not for the weak, as you will be surrounded by hundreds of other people walking the sidewalks and standing in lines.

Did You Know?

Austin was originally named Waterloo but was renamed when Congress decided whatever capital was selected for Texas would be named after Stephen F. Austin (18 fascinating facts about Austin, 2018).

Austin is classified as both Urban and Suburban depending on which area of Austin you're looking at.

Austin is the 7th capital of Texas (18 fascinating facts about Austin, 2018).

Austin is the home of the Barton Springs Salamander (18 fascinating facts about Austin, 2018).

Austin was incorporated in 1839 (Texas State Historical Association, 2020).

Austin attracts roughly 20 million visitors per year (18 fascinating facts about Austin, 2018).

Hot Spots to Visit
South Congress Bridge

Each year from March-September nearly 1.5 million Mexico Free-Tailed bats seek refuge under the South Congress Bridge in Austin, putting on a show for all to see each afternoon. Tourists will line the bridge and watch the bats take flight, putting on a show of dynamic aerial flight patterns (Austin Bats: Austin, Texas) in their hunt for insects.

Barton Springs

Barton Springs Pool is one of Austin's is unlike any other public pool in Austin. It measures roughly 3 acres across and is fed from a natural underground spring (Barton Springs Pool, 2023). The water temperature is roughly 68-70 degrees year-round, providing the perfect swimming conditions no matter the weather!

Franklin's BBQ

If you're a BBQ fan, Franklin's BBQ is one of a kind, literally, there's only one! Located in the heart of Austin, this establishment is among the top favorites of both natives and visitors alike in Austin. They're only open for lunch from 11 am-3 pm and operate on a first-come-first-serve basis, so it's not uncommon to see people camping in line starting hours before opening time to get in on this cuisine.

Though these places are some of the crown jewels of Austin, the music scene is what this city's really known for. Aside from the live music in bars and people playing on 6th Street, you'll want to visit one of these top 5 live music festivals to get the full experience:

  1. Austin City Limits (ACL)
  2. South By South West (SXSW)
  3. JMBLYA
  4. Rockfest
  5. iHeart Country Music Festival
